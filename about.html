Meet Pikstein of Pikstein Lab!

Rachel Pikstein is an environmental biologist, ecologist, and herpetologist originally from Oakland County, Michigan, who relocated to Phoenix, Arizona, in 2016. She served as a Biology Professor at Grand Canyon University and the University of Dubuque for seven years before beginning her PhD in Biology at Loma Linda University under the mentorship of Dr. Bill Hayes in 2024.

Established in 2023, Pikstein Laboratory for Conservation Research & Education operates as an independent research entity while maintaining its partnership with the Grand Canyon University Research & Design Program. The lab is dedicated to addressing critical environmental challenges—including biodiversity and habitat loss, invasive species, and other forms of ecological degradation.

Through academic mentoring (spanning K–12 to graduate-level students), regional, national, and global fieldwork, and the publication of peer-reviewed research, the Pikstein Lab advances science-based conservation solutions. Additionally, the lab fosters innovation through interdisciplinary collaboration and engages in diverse public outreach initiatives designed to cultivate a strong “conservation ethic” within individuals and communities.
